About the Author

A. R. Vance spent a decade designing the kind of infrastructure most people never think about — power grids, traffic systems, the invisible machinery that keeps a city breathing. Then one winter night the lights went out across an entire borough, and the only thought that wouldn't leave was a question: what if someone did that on purpose?

Zero Hour: New York is the answer to that question — a thriller built from the inside out by someone who knows exactly how fragile "unbreakable" really is. Vance writes the way the work feels: fast, technical, and one bad decision away from disaster.

Born in the Midwest and now based a few blocks from the river in New York, Vance writes mostly at night, fueled by black coffee and the hum of a city that never quite powers down. The Beginning is the first book in the Zero Hour series.

"I didn't want to write about a hero who saves the city. I wanted to write about the person who realizes it can't be saved — and decides what to do anyway." — A. R. Vance
