Synopsis

The Blackout

New York City doesn't fall apart in flames. It fractures — quietly, surgically, by design.

When a coordinated blackout freezes the city in seconds, the failure exposes more than vulnerable infrastructure. It reveals a system stretched past the point of trust, a population pushed beyond the point of patience, and a power struggle already underway beneath the streets.

Marcus Vance, a middle school teacher, finds himself at the center of the crisis — protecting students while becoming an unintended symbol of accountability. Ash, a brilliant and disciplined insurgent, applies pressure not to destroy the city, but to force it to confront what it has abandoned. Mayor Lyla Serrano, elected on hope, faces the cost of compromise as her reforms stall. And Constance Drake, a billionaire power broker, responds to the threat not with fear — but with a private war designed to preserve control at any cost.

As federal forces close in and the city holds its breath, New York must decide whether this moment will break it — or remake it.

Zero Hour: New York is a fast-paced political thriller about power, pressure, and the dangerous moment when a city can no longer pretend everything is fine.
