Synopsis

The Blackout

It begins with silence. At 9:14 p.m., the most connected city on Earth loses every signal at once — no power, no phones, no warning. For most of the eight million people in Manhattan, it's an inconvenience. For Maya Cole, a disgraced systems engineer who built the very grid that just failed, it's a confession she recognizes instantly: this was deliberate.

Somewhere in the dark, a message is waiting. A countdown. Ninety minutes until something far worse than a blackout — and a demand that only Maya can answer. As panic spreads from the subway tunnels to the trading floors, she races across a city she no longer recognizes, chasing a ghost from her own past who knows exactly how she thinks.

Because the blackout wasn't the attack. It was the invitation.

Zero Hour: New York is a propulsive, real-time thriller about the night the rules disappeared — and the impossible choice waiting at the end of the clock.

Why "The Beginning"?

The blackout is patient zero. Zero Hour: New York — The Beginning is the opening book of a planned trilogy that follows the fallout across the city, the country, and the people who can never go back to who they were before 9:14. Book one stands completely on its own — but it ends with a door you'll want to walk through.
