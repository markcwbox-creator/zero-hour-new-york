The blackout was only the beginning

New York City doesn't fall apart in flames. It fractures — quietly, surgically, by design.

When a coordinated blackout freezes the city in seconds, the failure exposes more than vulnerable infrastructure. It reveals a system stretched past the point of trust, a population pushed beyond the point of patience, and a power struggle already underway beneath the streets.
